BOX OFFICE

Avengers: Infinity War grossed $678.8 million in the United States and Canada, and $1.370 billion in other territories, for a worldwide total of $2.048 billion.[6] It became the fourth highest-grossing film of all time,[175][176] as well as the highest-grossing film of 2018,[177] the highest-grossing MCU film, and the highest-grossing superhero film.[178] Its worldwide opening weekend of $640.5 million is the biggest of all time, beating The Fate of the Furious' (2017) $541.9 million.[163] It was the fastest film to make $1 billion at the worldwide box office, doing so in 11 days to eclipse Star Wars: The Force Awakens' (2015) record of 12 days.[179] Also in its second weekend, Infinity War passed $13.5 million from 4DX screens, which was the all-time record for the format.[165] On June 12, 2018, Avengers: Infinity War passed the $2 billion mark at the worldwide box office, becoming the fourth film to cross that milestone after Avatar (2009), Titanic (1997), and The Force Awakens.[176] Crossing the mark in 48 days, it was the second-fastest to that point, after Avatar's 47-day record.[180] With $140 million worldwide from IMAX,[181] the film is the third-largest worldwide in the format, behind Avatar and The Force Awakens,[175] and the largest for a Marvel film.[182] In May 2018, two weeks after its release, Deadline Hollywood deduced the film had already broken even and estimated its net profit would be around $600 million. This value took into account production budgets, P&A, talent participations, and other costs, with box office grosses and ancillary revenues from home media.[3]

PRE-SALE TICKET RECORDS

In December 2017, a survey from Fandango indicated that Infinity War was the most anticipated film of 2018.[183] Fandango later reported that Infinity War achieved the largest initial 24-hour ticket pre-sales for a superhero film, taking just 6 hours to surpass the previous 24-hour record set by Black Panther. Atom Tickets reported that Infinity War sold more tickets in its first pre-sales day than Black Panther sold in its first month.[184] Within 72 hours, the film generated the largest pre-sales for any superhero film at AMC Theatres, with its advance ticket sales being 257.6% ahead of Black Panther's, 751.5% ahead of Captain America: Civil War, and 1,106.5% ahead of Avengers: Age of Ultron during the same time frame.[185] Two weeks before its release, Fandango revealed that advance ticket sales for Infinity War were outpacing the last seven MCU films combined in the same timeframe, and had become the company's top April release. It was also on pace to become the top superhero film.[186] A week before the film released, The Wall Street Journal said the film had sold more than $50 million worth of advance tickets, behind only The Force Awakens and Star Wars: The Last Jedi (2017),[187][163] with Fandango reporting that more than 2,500 showtimes had sold out. On Atom Tickets, Infinity War had the most pre-sale volume, selling 7% higher than The Last Jedi and 250% higher than Black Panther at the same point in their sales. Atom also reported that ticket sales for Infinity War were doubling daily the week of its release, the fastest rate of increase the service has seen for any MCU title.[163]

UNITED STATES AND CANADA

Avengers: Infinity War earned $106.7 million on its opening day in the United States and Canada (including $39 million from Thursday night previews), for an opening weekend total of $258.2 million. The Thursday night preview earning was the best for an MCU film (beating Avengers: Age of Ultron's $27.6 million) and the fourth-best of all time, behind The Force Awakens ($57 million), The Last Jedi ($45 million) and Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows â€“ Part 2 (2011) ($43.5 million). Fandango reported that $14 million of the $39 million came from ticket pre-sales from the company. The opening day gross was the second-best ever, behind The Force Awakens' $119.1 million; its Saturday gross of $83 million was the best-ever Saturday gross, beating Jurassic World's (2015) $69.6 million; and its Sunday gross of $69.2 million was the best-ever, beating The Force Awakens' $60.5 million. The total weekend gross became the highest-grossing opening weekend, beating The Force Awakens' $248 million. IMAX contributed $22.5 million to the opening weekend gross, which was the best opening for a Marvel film in the format and the third-biggest opening, behind The Force Awakens ($30 million) and The Last Jedi ($24.7 million). AMC reported that the film had the highest Friday and Saturday box office gross for a single title in the company's history, while Fandango reported that approximately $84 million worth of tickets were sold through the service, approximately 30%, which was the largest share of the weekend box office for any film in the company's history.[163] Avengers: Infinity War earned an additional $25 million the Monday after its opening weekend, which was the highest grossing Monday in April, beating Furious 7 (2015) ($14 million), and the second-best Monday gross for an MCU film, after Black Panther ($40.1 million).[188] The next day, it earned $23.5 million, which was the highest grossing Tuesday for an MCU film, beating Black Panther ($20.8 million), and the highest grossing Tuesday in May, beating The Avengers ($17.6 million). It also tied The Force Awakens for the fastest to reach $300 million at five days.[189]

The film remained number one in its second weekend, earning $115.5 million, which was the second-best second weekend ever after The Force Awakens ($149.2 million). Infinity War also surpassed $400 million in the weekend, doing so in nine days, becoming the second fastest film to reach that mark, again after The Force Awakens's eight days.[165] In its third weekend, Infinity War remained number one at the box office, and became the second-fastest film to surpass $500 million, doing so in 15 days (behind The Force Awakens's 10 days). The weekend also saw its total domestic IMAX gross become $48.1 million, which was the highest for any MCU film.[178] The film's fourth weekend saw it come in second, behind Deadpool 2,[190] and in its fifth it finished third behind Solo: A Star Wars Story and Deadpool 2.[191] By May 23, Infinity War surpassed $600 million, becoming the second-fastest film to do so in 26 days, after The Force Awakens (12 days).[192] It remained in the top ten through its ninth weekend.[193] In the United States and Canada, it went on to become the fourth highest-grossing film of all time,[176] and the second highest-grossing superhero film, behind Black Panther.[194]

OTHER TERRITORIES

Outside the United States and Canada, the film earned $382.7 million from 52 markets, opening number one in all, and became the number two opening internationally, behind The Fate of the Furious ($444.2 million). IMAX contributed $18.5 million, which was the best opening outside of the US, Canada, and China, surpassing The Force Awakens ($17.5 million).[195] All-time opening weekend records were set in South Korea ($39.2 million), Mexico ($25.4 million), Brazil ($19.1 million), India (for a Western release, $18.6 million), the Philippines ($12.5 million), Thailand ($10 million), Indonesia ($9.6 million), Malaysia,[195] Hong Kong, Vietnam,[196] Central America, Peru, Chile, Ecuador, Venezuela, Bolivia, South Africa, Turkey, the United Arab Emirates, and West Africa,[195] with many also setting opening day records.[197] France had the biggest superhero opening in the market with $17.7 million,[195] as did Denmark, Finland, Norway, Portugal, and Sweden.[196] The United Kingdom earned $8.9 million, which was the biggest MCU opening day and third highest opening day for a Disney film;[196] it would go on to earn $41.4 million for the weekend, which was the third-highest of all time and the second-highest Disney opening.[195] Argentina had the second highest opening day of all time, while Germany had the best superhero film opening day of all time,[196] ultimately earning $14.7 million, which was the best superhero opening ever.[195] Many other European countries and the Middle East also broke superhero film records.[196] Japan had the second-biggest opening for an MCU film with $9 million,[195] and Australia had the second-highest opening weekend ever, with $23.2 million.[195]

Avengers: Infinity War remained number one in its 54 markets in its second weekend.[166] The $4.9 million opening day in Russia was the biggest of all time. Infinity War was also the first film in Russia to sell more than 1 million tickets in a single day,[198] and went on to gross $17.6 million in the market, a new opening weekend record. IMAX contributed $2.2 million in the market, which was also an opening weekend record.[166] In its third weekend, the film remained number one in a majority of its markets. Infinity War opened in China to $200 million (RMB 1.266 billion), which was the second highest opening weekend in local currency behind The Fate of the Furious ($184 million and RMB 1.352 million). IMAX contributed $20.5 million, which was the third-largest opening in China.[182] Infinity War also broke China's pre-sale record of 400 million yuan ($63 million).[199] In India, Infinity War became the first Hollywood film to earn over â‚¹2 billion net ($29.7 million),[200] and also became the highest grossing MCU film in the United Kingdom.[201] In its sixth weekend, the film became the highest grossing MCU film in Japan with $33 million.[202] The film became the highest grossing release ever in Brazil, Indonesia, the Philippines, Central America, Bolivia, Venezuela, Latin America as a region, Mexico, Chile, Ecuador, Peru, Malaysia, Singapore, India (for a Western release), Vietnam, Indonesia, Thailand (for a Western release), and Mongolia,[166][182][175][181] the second-highest in the Asia-Pacific region (for a Western release), Hong Kong, and South Korea (for a Western release),[181] and the third-highest in China (for a Western release).[181] It is also the highest-grossing superhero film in many European countries, including the United Kingdom.[181] As of June 17, 2018, the film's top markets are China ($373.4 million), the United Kingdom ($95.7 million), and South Korea ($92.8 million).[203] The film is the third highest-grossing film of all time in territories outside the United States and Canada.[176]
